The Problem

E-commerce UI in healthcare

Every major diagnostic app treats test booking like shopping — strikethrough prices, discount badges, Add to Cart. This causes real medical harm.

The Insight

Info exists. Guidance doesn't.

Apps provide test descriptions. The gap: none guide users toward the clinically correct choice. Being informed is not the same as being guided.

The Solution

Clarity before commerce

ClearTest gates every booking behind understanding. Users feel confident — not anxious — because they know exactly what they booked and why.

01 — The Problem

When booking a test feels like
buying a shirt.

04 — Ideation & Design Decisions

What we didn't build,
and why it matters.

Most health-tech products compete on speed. ClearTest made the opposite bet — health decisions should require conscious effort, not less of it.

✕

AI-powered recommendations

Clinical authority belongs to the doctor. We surface the prescription — not an AI's interpretation.

✕

Prescription scanning / auto-fill

Removes conscious engagement. The reading moment is the most important. We protect it.

✕

1-click booking

Speed optimisation in healthcare isn't a feature — it's a liability. Friction here is intentional.

✕

Price-first layout

Anchors decisions to cost before understanding. The cheapest option is rarely clinically correct.

🖼️ Medical image before price

The test detail screen leads with an anatomical visual — making the abstract clinical concept tangible before any ₹ appears on screen.

⚖️ "Skip & Book Now" as friction

The secondary CTA uses the word "Skip" — users must actively choose to bypass understanding. It's impossible to miss accidentally.

📊 Transparent pricing only

Test Cost + GST + Pickup. No strikethroughs, no percentage badges. Price is informational here, not persuasive.

✓ Test name as confirmation headline

The final screen names the test prominently — one last moment to catch a mistake before a collection agent arrives.

06 — Outcomes & Reflection

What success
looks like.

🧪

Correct test booked

Primary success metric. Users book the test the doctor prescribed, not the cheapest one that sounds similar.

😌

Confidence post-booking

Users feel certain, not anxious, after booking. That's the emotional goal of every screen in ClearTest.

🔄

Return bookings

Users who return aren't coming for deals. They're coming because they trust the product.

What I'd do differently

The biggest gap is the absence of first-hand user research. Given more time, the priority would be observing real people booking tests in the actual moment — not in controlled interviews.

  • What makes users choose the cheaper test over the prescribed one?
  • Do users actually feel anxious after booking on current apps?
  • At what point in the flow does confidence drop most sharply?

What this taught me

When designing for something that affects health, honesty is not a design choice — it's a responsibility. Every screen should feel like it's on the user's side.

Removing quick booking and leading with explanation felt counterintuitive at first. But for products that handle sensitive decisions, usefulness outlasts attractiveness.

ClearTest v2

The next version moves beyond booking into health maintenance — proactively notifying users when it's time to retest based on their history. From a booking tool to a personal health companion.

07 — AI Use Disclosure

Honest about
how AI was used.

Claude AI was used in specific, bounded ways. All design thinking, problem framing, solution decisions and visual design were entirely human-led. Here's exactly what AI helped with — and what it didn't.

✓ AI Used

Clarifying medical terminology

Used Claude to translate complex test descriptions into plain language. AI translated jargon — it did not make design decisions.

✓ AI Used

Research & evidence gathering

Used Claude to locate the Mrazek et al. paper and synthesise academic literature on diagnostic booking behaviour. All sources verified independently.

✓ AI Used

Interactive prototyping

Used Claude to build this case study — translating Figma designs into browser code. Code generation, not design generation.

✕ Not AI

Problem identification

The insight about e-commerce patterns in medical apps came from direct observation of real apps and real user behaviour — not from AI prompting.

✕ Not AI

Solution decisions

Every design decision — the stage-gated flow, leading with understanding, removing quick booking, using "Skip" as friction — came from design reasoning.

✕ Not AI

Visual design

All Figma screens — colour system, typography, layout, component design, spacing — were designed entirely by hand. No generative visuals.

Why disclose this? Because honesty is the entire thesis of this project. ClearTest is about giving users the clarity to make the right decision. It would be inconsistent to be anything less than fully transparent about the tools used to build it.
